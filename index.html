Alan Shearer was born in Gosforth, Newcastle upon Tyne on the 13th of August 1970. He spent 18 years playing football as a center forward at the highest possible level. Starting as a youth at Wallsend Boys Club he made his professional debut at Southampton aged 17 before moving on to Blackburn Rovers where he won the Premier League Title. Finally, Alan moved to his boyhood team of Newcastle United where he spent the last 10 years of his playing career and became club captain. Alan also represented England on 63 occasions, scoring 30 goals and captaining the team in 34 of his games. He amassed a total of 734 professional appearances, scoring 379 times. He still holds the record for the most Premier League goals scored (260) plus the most Premier League hat-tricks in one season (5) as well as all-time Premier League hat-tricks (11).

Alan retired from professional football at the end of the 2006 season and played his last professional game on 17th April 2006. Following Alan's retirement, Newcastle United hosted a testimonial match to celebrate his 10 years at the club. A sell out crowd attended the game and witnessed Alan score a penalty. Following retirement, Alan found work in the media where he has become a regular on the BBC's Match of the Day. Alan now also helps out various charities on a local and national basis. He has also founded his own foundation, The Alan Shearer Foundation which is set up to support the Alan Shearer Centre in Newcastle: "a highly specialist, disability, respite, residential and social provision for people with complex disabilities and acute sensory impairments".

Alan was awarded an OBE (Officer of the Order of the British Empire) in the 2001 Queens Birthday Honors.
